Some folk dances from UP include:

This is a folk dance from Brij region of Uttar Pradesh. This is done by girls who wear dresses like peacocks, resplendent with peacock feathers and a glittering head-dress complete with a beak. This beak can be opened and closed with the help of a thread tied to it, and manipulated from within the dress. This dance is associated with Lord Krishna and Radha. It is considered that when Radharani wanted to see Mayur Nritya, Lord Krishna used to portray himself as a peacock and perform dance like Mayur.

The Raslila is the most popular form of folk dance of India, especially during the festivals of Krishna Janmashtami and Holi in the regions of Mathura and Vrindavan in Uttar Pradesh. This dance form is based on a traditional story of Krishna described in Hindu Scriptures such as Bhagavata Purana. Ras Lila is a popular form of folk theatre in the regions of Mathura, Vrindavana in Uttar Pradesh, especially during the festivals of Krishna Janmashtami and Holi, and amongst various followers of Gaudiya Vaishnavism in the region.

Kathak is one of the major classical dances of India. Kathak originated from Northern India through a group of Nomadic Bards. Katha means “Story telling” and Kathak means “art of story telling”. So Kathak is a dance that tells a story through alternative passages of mime and dance.
